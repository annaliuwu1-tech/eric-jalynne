Social Challenge

The Massive Gap and Social Pain Points in Stroke Rehabilitation

The global burden of stroke is concentrated in low-income regions. Traditional rehabilitation solutions are either prohibitively expensive or ineffective, depriving tens of millions of patients of the chance to regain their ability to live independently.

Large Patient Population

There are 20 million stroke patients in China each year, over 70% of whom are from rural and lower-tier cities. They face hand dysfunction but lack access to effective rehabilitation pathways.

Catastrophic Medical Expenses

71% of low-income households spend over 30% of their total income on stroke rehabilitation. High-end rehabilitation robots cost over $100,000, far beyond the reach of ordinary families.

Uneven Distribution of Medical Resources

Remote areas lack professional rehabilitation institutions and therapists. Patients must travel long distances for treatment, making high-frequency rehabilitation training difficult to achieve.

Market Gap: The Missing Middle-Tier Intelligent Rehabilitation Devices

The current market is polarized: high-end devices ($100k+) are powerful but expensive, suitable only for large hospitals; low-end assistive devices ($50-200) lack intelligence and adaptability, with limited rehabilitation effects. AeroGrip precisely fills this gap, offering hospital-grade rehabilitation experience at a price point of $125.

Solution

Aerospace-Grade Technology for Affordable Intelligent Rehabilitation

AeroGrip Intelligent Hybrid Finger Exoskeleton integrates deep learning and pneumatic actuation technology to simulate personalized guidance from professional therapists, enabling patients to complete precise, efficient rehabilitation training at home.

Core Technology: CNN-LSTM-PID Hybrid Control System

Our patented technology deeply integrates Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN), Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, and Proportional-Integral-Derivative (PID) controllers. It can accurately capture patients' neuromotor intentions and provide adaptive rehabilitation assistance—full support when weak, resistance when strong—simulating the professional judgment of human therapists throughout.

Business Model

Dual-Track Model Balancing Social Value and Commercial Sustainability

We adopt a B2C+B2B dual-track business model that makes our solution affordable for ordinary families while providing cost-effective rehabilitation solutions for medical institutions, achieving a win-win between social value and commercial returns.

Diversified Business Model

We build a sustainable revenue structure through a combination of direct sales and subscription services. 60% of revenue comes from direct product sales, and 40% from software subscription services, ensuring the company's long-term stable development.

B2C Retail Model

Direct sales to patient families through e-commerce platforms, doctor recommendations, and health community marketing, priced at $125 per unit to ensure affordability for all.

B2B Partnership Model

Cooperation with community hospitals and rehabilitation clinics to provide subscription-based rehabilitation services, enabling medical institutions to expand service capabilities without large upfront investments.
